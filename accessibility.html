Accessibility Statement

Effective Date: May 1, 2026  ·  Last Reviewed: May 8, 2026

Conformance Target: WCAG 2.1 Level AA

Our Commitment

Ryan & Lauren LLP is committed to ensuring that our website is accessible to all individuals, including those with disabilities. We believe that equal access to legal information and services is a fundamental right, and we strive to meet or exceed the requirements of:

  • The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), Title III
  • The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA
  • Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973

Measures We Have Taken

To support accessibility, Ryan & Lauren LLP has implemented the following features across our website:

  • Skip navigation link to allow keyboard users to bypass repeated header content
  • Semantic HTML5 landmarks including <main>, <header>, <nav>, <footer>, and <section>
  • ARIA roles, labels, and live regions on all interactive and dynamic content
  • Descriptive alt text on all images
  • Visible keyboard focus indicators on all interactive elements
  • Logical heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3) for screen reader navigation
  • All form fields have associated <label> elements
  • Colour contrast ratios meeting or exceeding 4.5:1 (WCAG AA) for all body text
  • Animated content (client name marquee) includes a visible Pause/Play control
  • Full prefers-reduced-motion support — all animations are disabled for users who have this OS-level setting enabled
  • Fully responsive layout for mobile and tablet viewports
  • No content relies solely on colour to convey meaning
  • All interactive elements are reachable and operable via keyboard alone

Known Limitations

While we strive for full WCAG 2.1 AA conformance, we acknowledge the following areas are actively being improved:

  • Some third-party content or embedded elements may not yet be fully accessible
  • Certain older browser or assistive technology combinations may experience minor inconsistencies

We are continuously auditing and improving our site's accessibility and aim to resolve any identified issues in a timely manner.

Technical Specifications

Our website was built using the following technologies, each of which contributes to accessibility:

  • HTML5 with semantic structure
  • CSS3 with prefers-reduced-motion and responsive design
  • Vanilla JavaScript with ARIA state management

The site has been tested with the following assistive technologies:

  • NVDA (NonVisual Desktop Access) with Firefox
  • VoiceOver with Safari (macOS and iOS)
  • TalkBack with Chrome (Android)
  • Keyboard-only navigation (all major browsers)
